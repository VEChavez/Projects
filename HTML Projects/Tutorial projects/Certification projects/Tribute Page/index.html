Teresa Magbanua y Ferraris

The woman known as the "Visayan Joan of Arc"

Teresa Magbanua's early life

Teresa Magbanua was a Filipina revolutionary who played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonization. Born on October 13, 1868, in Pototan, Iloilo, Philippines to affluent parents. Don Juan Magbanua, Teresa's father who is a a judge, sat on the Court of First Instance in Iloilo City, while Doña Alejandra Ferraris, Teresa's mother, is the daughter of Captain Benito Ferraris. Teresa is the second child of the Magbanua's Joining her sisters María and Paz, and younger brothers Manuel, Pascual and Elias.

Teresa Magbanua studied teaching in college, firstly at the Colegio de San José in Jaro, Iloilo, and later in Manila where she studied at three different all-girl's schools which are: Colegio de Santa Rosa, Santa Catalina College, and Colegio de Doña Cecilia. She earned her teaching certificate at Collegio de Doña Cecilia in 1894 and her masters soon after at University of Santo Tomas.

After her earning her teaching certificate and masters, she then returned home to Pototan to begin teaching, where her students knows her as a disciplinarian. After four years of teaching in Pototan. Magbanua moved to Sara, Iloilo and became a schoolteacher there.

Teresa Magbanua during the Philippine revolution

When the war broke out between the Filipinos and Spanish in 1896, Teresa Magbanua became a member of the Katipunan revolutionaries. While the Katipunan largely excluded women from the revolutionary army, they still recognized the important role of women in the struggle against the spanish and because of this, the Katipunan organized a women's chapter in the year 1893.

On October 1898, the war has broken out in the Iloilo province and her two brothers has joined the revolutionary army. Magbanua wanted to help in the war effort but her husband was against it however, Magbanua did not listen and went to her uncle, Major General Perfecto Poblador. Magbanua asked him to let her join the army and she would later take charge of the administrative division. And while hesitant at first, General Poblador gave Magbanua command of a battalion of bolo troops.

During the revolution, Magbanua fought in multiple significant battles during the revolution. On December 3, 1898, Magbanua's forces defeated the spanish forces at the battle of Barrio Yating which took place in Pilar, Capiz. She led her troops in her first battle on horseback. In the battle of Yating, Magbanua earned the nickname of "Visayan Joan of Arc" due to her efforts in the battle. Another nickname Magbanua had is "Nanay Isa" or "'Nay Isa".

During the same month, December 1898, Magbanua led her troops against the spanish forces at the battle of sapong hills. Despite the odds being against her, she had won the battle. After the battle, her forces were joined by revolutionary forces from Antique, led by General Leandro Fullon in an advance toward Iloilo City in the hopes of liberating the City, and on December 24, 1898, Magbanua participated in the liberation of Iloilo City wherein alongside Generals Martin Delgado, Roque Lopez, Quintin Salas and others, They successfully liberated the City of Iloilo from the spanish.

Teresa Magbanua during the Philippine-American war

During the Philippine–American War, Magbanua fought in many battles against American soldiers, as did her brothers. Both of her brothers were ranking officers, Pascual was a General while Elias was a Major. On February 11, 1899, Magbanua fought in the second battle of Iloilo City. Along with General Martin Delgado, they defended Iloilo City against the American forces. Sadly, the battle ended in defeat wherein the American forces successfully took Iloilo City without a single casualty reported.

On March 10, 1899, Magbanua participated in the Battle of Balantang, also known as the Second Battle of Jaro, alongside her brother, General Pascual. In this battle, they emerged victorious against the American forces, retaking Jaro. On the same year, Magbanua participated in the defense of the Balantang-Tacas-Jiabo-an line.

On December 1899, Teresa's brother General Pascua Magbanua died at the age of 24 under mysterious circumstances. In the same year, Major Elias Magbanua also died under mysterious circumstances at the age of 19. Both deaths dealt a blow to Teresa. And after the fall of the regional headquarters of the Filipino forces in Santa Barbara in the hands of the Americans, She began using guerrilla tactics. She unfortunately surrendered her troops in the year 1900 and after that, she then returned to farming.

Teresa Magbanua during World War II and later life and death

While she was not an active fighter during World War II, Magbanua did everything in her power to resist against the Japanese forces during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines. She sold her belongings to purchase food and supplies for the local guerrillas. After the war borke out, her husband, Alejandro Balderas died. Magbanua then sold their property in Iloilo to help in funding the guerrillas fighting against the Japanese.

After the end of World War II, Magbanua moved to Pagadian City, Zamboanga del Sur in Western Mindanao where she lived with her sister Maria. She never remarried after the death of Balderas and their marriage produced no children. Teresa Magbanua died on an unknown age in August of 1947. Her burial was only attended by close friends.
